Standardization Guidelines:
1. Use Title Case for Standardization:
Capitalize the first letter of each word except prepositions, conjunctions, and articles (e.g., 'and', 'of', 'to') unless they begin the term.
Examples:
 'daily' → 'Per Day'
  'twice weekly' → 'Twice Weekly'

2. Replace Numeric Abbreviations with Words: Convert numeric abbreviations into full words for clarity and consistency.
Examples:
 '3 times per week' → 'Three Times Per Week'
 '5x daily' → 'Five Times Per Day'

3. Standardize Frequency Descriptions: Unify variations of frequency terms to a consistent format. Replace 'weekly,' 'per week,' 'a week,' and '/week' with 'Per Week.' Similarly, standardize 'daily,' 'per day,' '/day' to 'Per Day.'

Examples:
'Three times a week' → 'Three Times Per Week'
'6 hours/day' → 'Six Hours Per Day'
'2x-Daily' → 'Two Times Per Day' 
'3x weekly' → 'Three Times Per Week'

4. - Use 'Once Per Day/Week' or 'Twice Per Day/Week' for frequencies of one and two.

- For frequencies of more than 5, keep the numerical number directly and use 'X Times Per Day/Week. '
Examples: 
Once daily → Once Per Day
weekly → Once Per Week    
Twice weekly→ Twice Per Week
3x weekly → Three Times Per Week
4 times daily → Four Times Per Day
5x weekly → Five Times Per Week
6 hours/day → 6 Hours Per Day

every 72 hours → Every 72 Hours

'three 45-minute sessions' → 'Three 45-Minute Sessions'

Examples:
'2 hourly' → 'Every Two Hours'
'8 hourly' → 'Every 8 Hours'
'15 cycles per minute' → '15 Cycles Per Minute'
              
7. Handling Combined Frequencies:
 Use '+' to separate combined frequencies. Standardize each frequency first, then rejoin with ' + '.
   Examples:

Examples:

 For repetitive or complex schedules, retain original details while ensuring clarity and consistency.
